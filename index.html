What is an FIR?

A First Information Report (FIR) is a written document prepared by the police when they receive information about the commission of a cognizable offense.

It is the first step in the legal process of crime investigation.

The FIR contains preliminary details about the crime, such as the date, time, place, and identity of the accused.

The police use the FIR to investigate the crime.

You can file an FIR for family disputes, commercial offenses, matrimonial disputes, corruption cases, medical malpractice cases, and more.

Why Should You File an FIR?

To report a crime and initiate an investigation.

To ensure legal action against the offender.

To safeguard your rights as a victim.

To serve as legal proof in court proceedings.

⚠️ Warning: Do Not File a False FIR

Filing a false FIR is a criminal offense and is punishable under *Section 182 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC)*.
Any false report may lead to legal consequences, including fines or imprisonment.
